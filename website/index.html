Our Mission


Studies estimate that over half of the world's seven thousand languages will likely be extinct by 2100. Unfortunately, there is a severe shortage of trained linguists with the expertise and wherewithal to document these endangered tongues before they vanish irrevocably. Linguists Without Borders provides digital tools and training resources for language documentation and curates a collective global repository of irreplaceable linguistic and cultural data. Our mission is to empower communities to preserve their own linguistic legacies, thus democratizing the process of language documentation and catalyzing a paradigm shift away from a limited cohort of professional linguists towards scalable, crowd-sourced data collection.

Our Story


Linguists Without Borders came into being as a gentle repudiation of traditional language documentation methods. While participating in more conventional field linguistics expeditions, the founders were dogged by a central question: to whom does a language belong? Certainly not solely to academics, but rather, to the people who speak it, and to humanity at large. Thus, we designed an app to guide community members through the process of endangered language documentation without formal training.

As we dug deeper into the field, it became clear that not only is there a pressing need to decolonize the tools and techniques of language documentation, it is also crucial to encourage cross-collaboration among existing documentation platforms -- particularly smaller-scale, local operations without a dedicated web presence. Doing so requires the standardization of data collection techniques and norms. Thus, what began as a simple mobile app has grown into full-fledged digital humanities enterprise, with the hope that linguists, non-linguists, NGO's, academic institutions, and communities all over the world will be equipped with the necessary tools to contribute to the canon of human knowledge.

Linguists Without Borders hopes to do for language documentation what Doctors Without Borders has done for medicine and Engineers Without Borders has done for development: erase artificial boundaries and democratize the generation and dissemination of knowledge. At Linguists Without Borders we believe that regardless of country, creed, or credentials, we all have the capacity to be linguists.

Our Technology


The Linguists Without Borders mobile app constitutes only a small portion of our enterprise, but it lies at the heart of our mission: empowering everyone, regardless of their linguistic background or formal education level, to participate in a global movement to preserve human knowledge. A user interacting with a linguistic community through the app will be prompted to elicit utterances in the target language. The resultant audio footage will be recorded, compressed, and stored in a local database, which can then be migrated to centralized servers for long-term storage and dissemination. The app comes pre-loaded with built-in vocabulary lists (selected by comparative linguists on the basis of their universality), but the user is free to upload her own custom lists as well. Grammatical templates prompt the user to ask for translations of specific utterances, which are deisgned to illuminate certain syntactic features of the language (such as word order, morphological markings, etc.). The app also provides the user with the opportunity record “free-form” audio, thereby facilitating the collection of songs, stories, proverbs, and other unstructured linguistic data.

The Linguists Without Borders mobile app was originally dubbed "DIL" for "Documenting Indigenous Languages," and is supported by an MIT IDEAS Global Challenge grant. In the video below (produced by the MIT PKG Center), DIL's founder discusses her vision for the project.

Where We Work


We ultimately hope to train and empower amateur linguists all over the world. At present, we are headquartered in Cambridge, Massachusetts with operations in Washington State, the American Southwest, and Gujarat, India.
