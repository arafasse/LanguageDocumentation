Our Mission


Studies estimate that over half of the world's seven thousand languages will likely go extinct by 2100. Unfortunately, the number of linguists with the wherewithal to document and preserve moribund languages is far from sufficient to meet the coming century's urgent demand. Linguists Without Borders provides digital tools and training resources for language documentation: specifically, we have developed a mobile application that guides non-linguists in applying formal linguistic practices towards the documentation and revitalization of vulnerable languages, automatically generating structured data for future study. The app provides elicitation templates for probing the syntactic features of a language, generates dictionaries, and offers a convenient way to record and store proverbs, songs, and stories.

Linguists Without Borders constitutes a significant departure from the current ad hoc, “ivory tower”-based approaches to language documentation and archival. We hope that this light-weight software tool will empower communities to document and preserve their own linguistic legacies, while also curating a collective global repository of irreplaceable linguistic and cultural data. Our mission is to democratize the process of language documentation and catalyze a paradigm shift away from reliance on a limited cohort of professional linguists towards scalable, crowd-sourced data collection.

Our Story


Linguists Without Borders came into being as a gentle repudiation of traditional language documentation methods. While participating in academically-driven field expeditions, the founders were dogged by a central question: to whom does a language belong? Certainly not solely to academics, but rather, to the people who speak it, and to humanity at large. Thus, we designed an app to guide community members through the process of endangered language documentation without formal training.

As we dug deeper into the field, it became clear that not only is there a pressing need to decolonize the tools and techniques of language documentation, it is also crucial to encourage collaboration and cross-talk among existing documentation platforms -- particularly smaller-scale, local operations without a dedicated web presence. Doing so requires the standardization of data collection techniques and norms. Thus, what began as a simple mobile app has grown into full-fledged digital humanities enterprise, with the hope that linguists, non-linguists, NGO's, academic institutions, and communities all over the world will be equipped with the necessary tools to contribute to the canon of human knowledge.

True to its name, Linguists Without Borders hopes to do for language documentation what Doctors Without Borders has done for medicine and Engineers Without Borders has done for development: erase artificial boundaries and democratize the generation and dissemination of knowledge. At Linguists Without Borders we believe that regardless of country, creed, or credentials, we all have the capacity to be linguists.

Our Technology


The Linguists Without Borders mobile app constitutes only a small portion of our enterprise, but it lies at the heart of our mission: empowering everyone, regardless of their linguistic background or formal education level, to participate in a global movement to preserve human knowledge.

An untrained user interacting with a linguistic community through the app is prompted to elicit utterances in the target language. The resultant audio footage is recorded, compressed, and stored in a local database, which is subsequently migrated to centralized servers (once an internet connection is available) for long-term storage and dissemination. The application's lexical elicitation protocol starts off with the Swadesh List (a set of roughly 200 basic vocabulary words selected by comparative linguists on the basis of their universality), although the user is free to generate her own custom vocabulary lists as well. Grammatical elicitation templates then prompt the user to elecit specific utterances, which will illuminate certain syntactic features of the language (such as word order, plural formation, morphological markings, etc.). The app also provides the user with the opportunity to record “free-form” audio, thereby permitting the collection of songs, stories, proverbs, and other unstructured linguistic data.

Planned functional enhancements to the app include:

  • The ability to compile all the data collected for a given language into a preliminary grammar and dictionary of the language for pedagogical and/or revitalization purposes.
  • Seamless integration with existing web-based archival platforms for language documentation, such as the People's Linguistic Survey of India.
  • Protocols for quantifying the relative “health” of a language, using an estimate of linguistic endangerment formalized by the EDGE (“evolutionarily distinct and globally endangered") metric from conservation biology.
  • The ability to calculate the most statistically probable rendering of each word and compile weighted lists of variants (possibly using machine learning techniques).

Where We Work


We ultimately hope to train and empower amateur linguists all over the world. At present, we are headquartered in Cambridge, Massachusetts with operations in Washington State, the American Southwest, and Gujarat, India.
